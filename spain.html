Basic Info

Spain is a vibrant and passionate country full of colourful traditions, mouthwatering food, and architectural marvels. From Gaudí’s designs in Barcelona to flamenco dances in Seville and the Alhambra in Granada, Spain offers a blend of modern and medieval. The Mediterranean beaches, bustling markets, and warm climate add to its appeal. Spain is also famous for tapas, football, festivals like La Tomatina and the Running of the Bulls.

Top Cities

  • Madrid – Capital with world-class museums
  • Barcelona – Gaudí’s architectural playground
  • Seville – Flamenco and Moorish history
  • Granada – Home to the Alhambra

Best Restaurants

Savor avant-garde cuisine at Disfrutar in Barcelona (~€150–250). Enjoy traditional tapas at Casa Lucio in Madrid (~€30–50). Budget travelers can find tasty meals at El Tigre (Madrid) with drinks + tapas for ~€10–15. Try seafood paella at Can Majó (~€40–70).

Hotels

Stay in luxury at Mandarin Oriental Barcelona or Hotel Ritz Madrid (~€350–600). Affordable boutique stays like Room Mate Hotels (~€100–200) are widely available. Budget hostels start at €20–50/night.

Flights

Major airports include Madrid-Barajas and Barcelona El-Prat. Flights from the UK and Europe cost €30–100. Domestic flights from Madrid to Barcelona, Seville, and Valencia range €40–80. High-speed trains (AVE) are also a great option.

Tourist Spots

  • Sagrada Familia – Barcelona
  • Alhambra Palace – Granada
  • Park Güell – Barcelona
  • Royal Palace – Madrid
  • La Rambla – Barcelona's lively boulevard
