Basic Info

Greece, the cradle of Western civilization, is known for its ancient history, stunning islands, and Mediterranean charm. From the ruins of Athens and Olympia to the whitewashed houses of Santorini and Mykonos, Greece blends mythology and breathtaking beauty. Visitors can enjoy sunny beaches, vibrant nightlife, and rich local cuisine. Its cultural legacy, combined with natural wonders and warm hospitality, makes Greece an enchanting destination year-round.

Top Cities

  • Athens – Historical heart of Greece
  • Thessaloniki – Cultural and foodie paradise
  • Patras – Port city with Roman landmarks
  • Heraklion – Gateway to Crete and Minoan sites

Best Restaurants

Taste Greek flavors at Funky Gourmet in Athens (~€120–200), or enjoy seafood at Varoulko Seaside (~€50–100). Budget meals like souvlaki and moussaka at O Thanasis cost around €10–20. Try Ta Karamanlidika Tou Fani for traditional mezze platters (€15–30).

Hotels

Stay at the luxurious Grande Bretagne in Athens (~€350–600/night) or Katikies in Santorini (~€400–700/night). Mid-range options like Electra Hotels (~€100–200/night) offer excellent service. Budget hostels and Airbnb options start at €30–60 per night.

Flights

Main international gateways include Athens International Airport (ATH) and Thessaloniki. Flights from Europe start at €50 with budget airlines. Domestic flights to islands like Crete and Rhodes (~€30–80) are frequent. Ferries are also a scenic alternative to flying.

Tourist Spots

  • Acropolis of Athens
  • Santorini Island – Caldera views & sunsets
  • Delphi – Ancient oracle and ruins
  • Meteora – Cliff-top monasteries
  • Crete – Beaches and ancient ruins
