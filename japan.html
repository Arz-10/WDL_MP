Basic Info

Japan is a harmonious fusion of futuristic innovation and timeless tradition. With ultra-modern cities like Tokyo and Osaka, and serene spots like Kyoto and Nara, it offers a multifaceted experience. From sushi bars and cherry blossoms to bullet trains and samurai heritage, Japan is a dream for culture, tech, and nature lovers alike. Politeness, precision, and pop culture add to the charm of the Land of the Rising Sun.

Top Cities

  • Tokyo – The bustling capital city
  • Kyoto – Famous for temples and geishas
  • Osaka – Known for street food and nightlife
  • Hokkaido – Great for skiing and nature

Best Restaurants

Dine at the world-famous Sukiyabashi Jiro (~¥30,000+ or ~$200+). Affordable yet amazing options include Ichiran Ramen (~¥1,000) and Gonpachi (inspiration for Kill Bill – ¥2,000–4,000). Conveyor belt sushi at Sushiro is fun and budget-friendly (~¥100–150/plate).

Hotels

High-end stays like The Peninsula Tokyo and Aman Kyoto (~¥50,000–100,000/night or $350–800). Mid-range hotels like Dormy Inn (~¥8,000–15,000). Budget travelers can opt for capsule hotels (~¥2,000–4,000) or business hotels (~¥5,000–7,000).

Flights

Japan’s major airports include Narita (NRT), Haneda (HND), and Kansai (KIX). Flights from Asia can be ~$200–400, while from Europe/US ~$700–1,200. Domestic airlines like Peach and ANA offer cheap flights (~¥5,000–12,000) between cities.

Tourist Spots

  • Mount Fuji – Japan’s iconic peak
  • Fushimi Inari Shrine – Kyoto’s famous torii gates
  • Tokyo Skytree – Futuristic tower with panoramic views
  • Arashiyama Bamboo Grove – Kyoto
  • Himeji Castle – UNESCO heritage site
