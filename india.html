Basic Info

India is a vibrant tapestry of culture, history, spirituality, and diversity. It is located in South Asia and is home to ancient civilisations, iconic landmarks like the Taj Mahal, bustling markets, tranquil backwaters, and majestic Himalayas. Each state offers its own language, food, and traditions, making travel in India a sensory delight. Whether exploring royal palaces in Rajasthan, beaches in Goa, or the spiritual city of Varanasi, India promises an unforgettable and enriching experience for every traveller.

Top Cities

  • Delhi – The bustling capital city
  • Jaipur – Known for its historic forts and palaces
  • Mumbai – India's financial capital
  • Goa – Famous for its beautiful beaches

Best Restaurants

Savour authentic Indian cuisine at Bukhara (Delhi, ~$40–60 per person) or enjoy modern takes at Indian Accent (Delhi/Mumbai, tasting menu ~$70–100). In the South, try Murugan Idli Shop (Chennai, ~$2–5 for local breakfast). Street food in cities like Mumbai and Kolkata can be enjoyed for under $1, offering delicious bites like vada pav and kathi rolls.

Hotels

Stay like royalty at Taj Lake Palace in Udaipur (~$400–700/night) or opt for elegant city stays at The Oberoi (Delhi, Mumbai – ~$250–400/night). Mid-range options include Lemon Tree or Ginger Hotels (~$40–80/night). Budget travelers can find reliable guesthouses or hostels for as low as $10–25/night, especially in tourist cities like Jaipur and Kochi.

Flights

Major airports include Indira Gandhi International (Delhi), Chhatrapati Shivaji Maharaj International (Mumbai), and Kempegowda International (Bangalore). International flights to India average ~$400–$900 depending on region. Domestic flights with IndiGo, SpiceJet, and Air India range from $25 to $120 per leg. Popular routes include Delhi to Goa, Mumbai to Kochi, and Jaipur to Varanasi.

Tourist Spots
