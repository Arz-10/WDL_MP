Basic Info

Egypt, located in North Africa, is a land of ancient wonders and enduring mystique. Home to the Great Pyramids of Giza and the Sphinx, it offers rich historical experiences alongside vibrant cities and desert landscapes. The Nile River flows through its heart, nourishing cities like Cairo and Luxor, while the Red Sea coast draws divers and beach lovers. With temples, tombs, colorful souks, and a blend of Arab and African culture, Egypt captivates travelers across the world.

Top Cities

  • Cairo – Capital with ancient and modern charm
  • Alexandria – Mediterranean city with historical depth
  • Luxor – Gateway to the Valley of the Kings
  • Aswan – Nile-side beauty and cultural richness

Best Restaurants

Enjoy traditional dishes at Abou El Sid in Cairo (~100–200 EGP), or seafood delights at Fishawi’s (~120–250 EGP). For a luxurious experience, try Sequoia on the Nile (~300–500 EGP per person). Budget travelers can savor delicious koshari or falafel for under 30 EGP from street stalls or local cafes like Gad.

Hotels

Stay at the lavish Four Seasons Nile Plaza in Cairo (~$300–500/night) or enjoy historical luxury at Old Cataract Hotel in Aswan (~$200–400/night). Budget options include Horus Hostel (~$20–40/night). Mid-range hotels like Steigenberger provide great comfort in Luxor and Cairo (~$80–150/night).

Flights

Cairo International Airport (CAI) is Egypt’s main air hub. Flights from Europe range from $200–600. Domestic flights with EgyptAir and Nile Air connect Cairo with Luxor, Aswan, and Hurghada (~$50–120 per leg). Red Sea resorts like Sharm El Sheikh are easily accessible via direct flights from Europe and the Middle East.

Tourist Spots

  • Great Pyramids of Giza – Ancient wonders of the world
  • Valley of the Kings – Tombs of the Pharaohs
  • Luxor Temple – Night-lit magnificence
  • Karnak Temple – Vast complex of ruins and columns
  • Abu Simbel – Carved temples of Ramses II
