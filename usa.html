Basic Info

The United States of America, often referred to as the USA or simply America, is a vast country located in North America comprising 50 diverse states. It spans coast to coast, offering a rich blend of culture, history, innovation, and natural beauty. Tourists flock here for its world-famous landmarks like the Statue of Liberty, the Grand Canyon, and Hollywood. From bustling metropolitan cities like New York and Los Angeles to tranquil national parks and scenic drives like Route 66, the USA promises something for every type of traveler, whether it's adventure, relaxation, education, or entertainment.

Top Cities

  • New York City – The city that never sleeps, famous for its skyline, Broadway shows, and diverse neighborhoods.
  • Los Angeles – Entertainment capital of the world, home to Hollywood, beaches, and cultural fusion.
  • Chicago – Known for its architecture, deep-dish pizza, jazz music, and vibrant art scene.
  • San Francisco – Famous for the Golden Gate Bridge, tech startups, and hilly streets.
  • Washington D.C. – The nation's capital with monuments, museums, and political significance.

Best Restaurants

Indulge in gourmet dining at Eleven Madison Park (New York, tasting menu ~$365), or experience fresh seafood at Joe's Stone Crab (Miami, ~$45–70 per meal). In Chicago, savor deep-dish pizza at Giordano's (~$15–25). For a taste of authentic Mexican in LA, head to Guelaguetza (~$20–35). The USA offers both Michelin-star elegance and casual street eats on every corner.

Hotels

Stay in iconic luxury at The Plaza Hotel (New York, ~$750/night), or enjoy comfort and skyline views at Hotel Nikko (San Francisco, ~$280/night). Mid-range travelers can opt for Hilton Garden Inn or Marriott Marquis (average ~$150–250/night). Budget options like La Quinta or Motel 6 (~$60–100/night) are also widely available near major attractions and highways.

Flights

A vast air network connects the USA. Major international airports include JFK (New York), LAX (Los Angeles), and ORD (Chicago O'Hare). Round-trip international flights average $600–$1,200 depending on season and origin. Domestic flights via Delta, United, or Southwest range from $80 to $300. Popular flight routes include NYC to LA, Chicago to Miami, and SF to Seattle.

Tourist Spots

  • Grand Canyon – A breathtaking natural wonder in Arizona.
  • Statue of Liberty – A symbol of freedom located in New York Harbor.
  • Yellowstone National Park – The first national park, filled with geysers, wildlife, and nature trails.
  • Times Square – Bright lights and big crowds in NYC’s entertainment district.
  • Hollywood Walk of Fame – Celebrating stars of the entertainment industry in Los Angeles.
  • Niagara Falls – A natural border between the US and Canada with massive waterfalls.
  • Disneyland & Disney World – Magical family experiences in California and Florida.
