Basic Info

Russia, the largest country in the world, stretches across Eastern Europe and northern Asia, offering a wealth of historical, cultural, and natural diversity. From the imperial architecture of St. Petersburg to the Kremlin in Moscow and the Trans-Siberian Railway, Russia presents unique adventures. With its world-class museums, snowy landscapes, traditional ballet, and rich literature, it’s a country of contrasts that invites deep exploration.

Top Cities

  • Moscow – Capital city, home to the Kremlin and Red Square
  • St. Petersburg – Cultural capital with canals and palaces
  • Kazan – Fusion of Russian and Tatar cultures
  • Sochi – Black Sea resort city

Best Restaurants

Dine in style at White Rabbit in Moscow (~₽4,000–8,000 or $45–90). Enjoy traditional Russian fare at Café Pushkin (~₽2,000–4,000). Budget meals like borscht or blinis at Teremok or Mu-Mu cost ₽300–600. Try Severyane for modern Russian cuisine (~₽1,500–3,000).

Hotels

Top-tier hotels like Four Seasons Moscow (~$400–700/night) and Belmond Grand Hotel Europe in St. Petersburg offer luxury. Budget hotels and hostels start at $25–50 per night. Mid-range options like Azimut Hotels and Novotel range from $80–150.

Flights

International flights arrive at Sheremetyevo (Moscow) and Pulkovo (St. Petersburg). European flights can be under $200. Domestic flights from Moscow to Siberia, Kazan, or Sochi range between $50–150. Russian Railways offer scenic train alternatives.

Tourist Spots

  • Red Square & Kremlin – Moscow
  • Hermitage Museum – St. Petersburg
  • Lake Baikal – World’s deepest lake
  • Trans-Siberian Railway – Epic train journey
  • Saint Basil’s Cathedral
