Basic Info

Australia, the Land Down Under, is a country and continent known for its unique wildlife, vibrant cities, and stunning natural wonders. From the iconic Sydney Opera House and Harbour Bridge to the rugged Outback and Great Barrier Reef, Australia blends modern urban living with rich Indigenous culture and breathtaking landscapes. Tourists enjoy laid-back beach towns, adventure sports, wildlife safaris, and top-notch wineries. Cities like Sydney, Melbourne, and Brisbane offer cosmopolitan charm, while destinations like Uluru and Tasmania showcase the untouched beauty and spirit of the land.

Top Cities

  • Sydney – Famous for the Opera House and beaches
  • Melbourne – Culture and coffee
  • Brisbane – Gateway to the Great Barrier Reef
  • Perth – Beautiful coastal city

Best Restaurants

Dine at Quay in Sydney for fine dining with views of the Opera House (tasting menu ~ $250). Melbourne’s Attica offers innovative cuisine (~$310 AUD). Seafood lovers must try Saint Peter (Sydney, ~$80–150 AUD). For budget eats, Lentil As Anything offers donation-based meals in multiple cities. Australia blends luxury and casual dining with local produce and flavors.

Hotels

Experience luxury at Park Hyatt Sydney (~ $800/night) or enjoy chic modernity at The Langham Melbourne (~$400 AUD/night). Budget-friendly choices include YHA Hostels and Ibis Styles (~$70–130 AUD/night). Family travelers love resorts like Sea World Resort (Gold Coast, ~$200–300 AUD). Whether beachside or urban, accommodation is easy to find for every budget.

Flights

Major international airports include Sydney (SYD), Melbourne (MEL), and Brisbane (BNE). Flights from Asia average ~$500–$800 AUD, while long-haul flights from Europe/US range from ~$1,200–$2,000 AUD. Domestic flights with Qantas, Jetstar, or Virgin Australia average ~$80–$200 AUD. Scenic air routes connect key destinations like Sydney to Cairns or Melbourne to Hobart.
